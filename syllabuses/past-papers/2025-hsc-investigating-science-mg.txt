Question 25 
Criteria Marks 
• Explains why the Mozart Effect is an example of misinterpreting 
causation as correlation 3 
• Describes feature(s) of causation and/or correlation 
AND/OR 
• Relates correlation to the Mozart Effect and distinguishes this from 
causation 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample answer: 
The Mozart Effect is a result of a study where participants who listened to Mozart were able 
to perform better in spatial reasoning tasks. The researchers saw the correlation between 
Mozart music and test results (as one increased, the other increased) and interpreted it as 
causation (Mozart music is improving test results). This ignores that there could be another 
reason for this correlation, such as general mood or attention level of the participants .
