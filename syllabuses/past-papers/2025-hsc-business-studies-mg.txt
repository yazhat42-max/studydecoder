Question  22  (b)  
 Criteria  Marks 
 •          Justifies TWO product strategies that could be implemented by the  
 business to gain a competitive advantage  4 
 •     Justifies ONE and outlines ONE product strategy that could be 
         implemented by the business to gain a competitive advantage  3 
 •    Justifies ONE product strategy that could be implemented by the 
 business 
 OR 
 •          Outlines TWO product strategies that could be implemented by the 
 business  2 
 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
The business could implement a new packaging strategy where the bottle packaging box is 
made from recyclable materials. This would persuade customers to purchase the product over competitor products by enhancing the ecofriendly features of the packaging. 
A branding strategy allows the product to be well known by the consumers. The business 
could include a modern logo and colour their bottles a distinct green which is synonymous with the environment. 
Implementing both strategies will allow the business to gain a competitive advantage through 
increased product awareness.

Question  23  (b)  
 Criteria  Marks 
 •      Demonstrates a comprehensive understanding of how comparative ratio 
    analysis can be used to improve the financial outcomes of the business  4 
 •         Demonstrates a sound understanding of how comparative ratio analysis 
    can be used to improve financial outcomes of the business  3 
 •    Shows some understanding of how comparative ratio analysis can be 
used   2 
 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
Ratios can be compared across different years, against industry standards or benchmarks. 
In this business, the expense ratio has declined; the business is not efficiently spending 
funds and can begin to control expenses which may improve profitability. 
Analysis of the gearing ratio and return on equity against industry averages shows the 
business is performing poorly. They may decide to use equity and not borrow more as they are already highly geared and taking more risks than others, which affects their long-term 
viability.

Question  23  (c)  
 Criteria  Marks 
 •          Explains how TWO global market influences could affect the solvency of 
 this business  4 
 •         Outlines TWO global market influences that affect the solvency of this 
 business  3 
 •      Outlines ONE global market influence 
 OR 
 •  Identifies TWO global market influences  2 
 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
This travel agency will be impacted by the global economic outlook. If the international 
economy is strong, the business may need more funds to grow as their travel service 
expands. If these funds are borrowed, it will further increase the debt and negatively impact 
solvency. 
If interest rates are decreasing, the amount borrowed overseas will have less interest 
expense and reduce the risks undertaken by the business, lowering the overall gearing of this business.

 from joining a union  3 
 •     Outlines TWO possible reason s 
 OR 
 •    Explains ONE possible reason  2 
 •     Provides some relevant information   1

Unions will advocate for higher pay and better conditions like leave and work health and 
safety processes. These changes are costly and will reduce the profits of the business. Employers may also discourage unionisation as it brings their processes under f urther

 illegal practices  4 
 •         Provides a sound explanation of how this business is engaging in illegal 
 practices  3 
 •       Demonstrates some understanding of illegal practices  2

Sample answer: 
Under Australian government laws it is illegal to discriminate and pay migrant workers less , 
as they may not be meeting minimum wage rates or in breach of awards and enterprise 
agreements. Bullying staff is illegal and could be in breach of antidiscrimination laws or work, 
health and safety. Businesses who undertake these actions can be taken to court and fined.
