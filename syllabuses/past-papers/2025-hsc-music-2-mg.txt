Sample  answer:  
Texture  is  used  to  create  interest  through  varying  densities.  For  example,  the  excerpt  begins 
with  solo  violin  and  monophonic  texture  (bars  1−2).  A second  accompaniment  layer  is added 
in  bars  3−4.  The texture is then intensified through a new l  ayer  by harpsichord,  which 
increases  the  pitch  range  and  louder  dynamic in bar  5 onwards.  
Answers  could include:  
Texture  is  used  in  the  following  ways:   
•  Monophonic  (bars  1−2)  –  Solo  violin,  stating  the  theme  
•  Homophonic  (bars  3−4)  –  An  accompaniment  violin  layer  is  added,  intensifying  the  texture  
slightly and chang es  the texture briefly to homophonic  
•  Harpsichord  adds  both  texture  density  through  increased  range,  and  ‘terraced dynamic ’  
effect  (ie  more  instruments,  louder  dynamic),  and  restates  the  theme  (bars  5−6),  along 
with  improvised  accompaniment,  based  on  the  provided  figures /harmonies  
•  Texture  is  increased  to  create  variety,  with  close  pitch  range  in  bar  3 (from uni  son,  
stepping wider)  to bar  11 has the widest  pitch range (3 octaves)  adding the textural  
density of  this climactic section  
•  Imitation of rhythm is seen to keep the texture consistent with a continual moving  
semiquaver  in each bar  (eg  harpsichord bars  5−6,  violin bar  7,  harpsichord bar  8,  
violin  bar  9)  
•  Tension/resolution  affects densi  ty over  cadence point  in bars  12−13  
•  There  is  polyphonic style movement  with staggered entry and independent  voice 
movement.

Duration  creates  unity  through:
