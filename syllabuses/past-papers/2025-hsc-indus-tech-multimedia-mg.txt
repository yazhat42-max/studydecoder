• Describes how analogue and digital video types differ 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:

natural  transitions.  Digital video  types  use  discrete  signals , allowing  better  quality,  easier  
editing and correction.

Question 14 
Criteria Marks 
• Explains the potential advantages of using motion capture technology in 
a short, animated film 4 
• Describes potential advantages of using motion capture technology in a 
short, animated film 3 
• Outlines potential advantages of using motion capture technology in a 
short, animated film 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
Motion  capture  (MoCap ) enhances animation by creating realistic, fluid movements while  
reducing the need for labour -intensive  keyframing.  It  improves  efficiency,  captures  subtle  
emotions and makes characters more expressive.  MoCap data is reusable,  adding long -term  
value.  Selectively using MoCap for  complex scenes balances cost  and quality,  making the 
film more engaging and marketable.  
Question 15 
Criteria Marks 
• Describes the ethical implications of image manipulation and 
enhancement 5 
• Describes some aspects of the ethical implications of image 
manipulation and enhancement 4 
• Outlines the ethical implications of image manipulation and enhancement 3 
• Identifies the ethical implications of image manipulation and 
enhancement 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
Image manipulation and enhancement in the multimedia industry raise ethical concerns  
regarding authenticity, misinformation and consumer deception. Over -editing can create 
unrealistic beauty standards,  impacting mental  health and self- perception.  In journa lism,  
altered images can mislead the public,  distorting facts and eroding trust.  Alternatively,  a  
positive ethical  example could be a newsworthy image of  a traumatic scene that  is pixelated 
or  blurred to protect  privacy.  Copyright  and  ownership  issues  arise when modifying images 
without  proper  attribution  or  consent.  Ethical  editing  should  balance  creativity  with  honesty,  
ensuring transparency in advertising and media.

Sample  answer:  
The  multimedia  industry  has  been  significantly  affected by  various historical  developments in 
manufacturing  processes.  During  the  late  20th  century,  the introduction of  digital  technology 
revolutioni sed the production and distribution of  multimedia content.  This shift  from  analogue  
to digital allowed for higher quality production, easier editing and faster distribution.  
 
In the 21st  century,  advancements in microprocessor  technology have further  transformed 
the industry. These developments have led to the creation of more powerful and compact 
devices,  such as smartphones and tablets,  which have become primary tools for  consuming 
multimedia  content.  The  miniaturi sation of  components has also allowed for  the integration of  
multiple  functions  into  single  devices,  enhancing  user  experience.  
 
The  rise  of  cloud  computing  has  had  a  profound  impact  on  the  multimedia  industry  as  well.  
Cloud  technology  enables  the  storage  and  streaming  of  vast  amounts  of  content,  making  it  
easily accessible to users worldwide.  This has led to the growth of  streaming  services like 
Netflix  and  Spotify,  which  have  revolutioni sed how m  ultimedia content  is consumed and 
distributed.  
 
These  historical  advancements  have  collectively  enhanced  the  efficiency,  accessibility  and  
quality of  multimedia production and distribution.  They have also opened up new possi  bilities 
for innovation and creativity, shaping the industry into what it is tod ay.  
Answers  could include:  
•  Mechanisation  and  automation  revolutionised  production,  increasing  efficiency  and  
reducing labour costs.  
•  Mass  production  methods  enabled  affordability  and  wider  accessibility  of  products.  
•  Digital  and  computer -aided technologies enhanced precision,  consistency and design 
flexibility.  
•  Miniaturisation  of  components  improved  functionality,  portability  and  material  efficiency.
