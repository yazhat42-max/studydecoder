medium   3

Aluminium is commonly used in transmission lines because its low density allows it to better 
support its own weight over long spans, reducing sag and enabling wider tower spacing. This 
makes infrastructure more economical with fewer towers required in the transmission 
network. Aluminium is also less expensive than copper and more abundant in nature.

Question  24 (c)  
 Criteria  Marks 
•  Describes  the  operation  of  a  mechanical  altimeter  
•  Supports  answer  with  a  relevant labelled  diagram    4 
•  Outlines  the operation of a mechanical altimeter  
•  Includes a  relevant labelled  diagram   3 
•  Describes  the operation of a mechanical altimeter  
OR  
•  Provides  a  relevant  labelled  sketch   2 
•  Provides  some  relevant  information   1 
Sample answer: 
An altimeter operates by measuring changes in atmospheric pressure as altitude changes. It 
uses a sealed capsule (aneroid) that responds to external air pressure. As the altitude 
increases, the external pressure connected to the static vent decreases, causing the sealed 
capsule to expand. This expansion is mechanically linked to a needle or a digital display, which indicates the altitude. The greater the altitude, the greater the expansion of the sealed 
capsule due to lower air pressure around the capsule.

heated to a specific temperature after being rapidly cooled from a high temperature, allowing 
dissolved alloying elements to precipitate out of the material, forming tiny particles that significantly increase the material's strength and hardness .
