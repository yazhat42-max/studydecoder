 Criteria  Marks 
 •        Compares skilfully how the composers of Text 4 and Text 5 explore 
     ideas about place, using well -selected textual evidence   5 
 •        Compares effectively how the compose rs of Text 4 and Text 5 explore 
       ideas about place, using appropriate textual evidence  4 
 •        Compares how the composers of Text 4 and Text 5 explore ideas about 
   place, using some textual evidence  3 
 •         Describes how Text 4 and/or Text 5 explore idea(s) about place, using 
   limited textual evidence  2 
 •        Provides some relevant information about the text(s)  1 
Sample answer: 
While Text 4 and Text 5 both explore the importance of place, Text 4 achieves this through a 
shared experience, and Text 5 shows how place fosters feelings of belonging for different 
individuals . 
Text 4 conveys how a physical place can establish a sense of community. Text 4 uses the 
walls to frame the people of ‘10 Pomegranate Street ’ allowing us to understand that they are 
united through the shared experience of gardening. In addition, their comfortable body 
language in caring for the garden reveals how working in a shared space creates a thriving 
community. 
On the other hand, Text 5 expresses a strong sense of belonging for people living in 
‘desolate parts’ . The composer spotlights Elliot’ s experience, where his confident tone in the 
repetition, ‘I belong here ’ and ‘I belong in Winton ’ emphasises his strong connection to his 
physical environment, contributing to his sense of home. This prompts Ewart to reflect on her 
memories of home, as she also grew up in rural Australia and the metaphoric ‘constant pull 
back to the community ’ contributes to developing our understanding that a connection to 
place can continue to last long after we are no longer there.
