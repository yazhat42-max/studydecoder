Sample answer: 
An important strategy for meeting the market specification of fat score 2 –3 for lambs is 
ensuring good nutrition right through until the sale of the lambs so they grow quickly and 
have an appropriate fat score at the chosen sale weight. 
This strategy is effective as lambs will have a high lean meat yield, desired carcase 
composition and good eating quality at the time of sale. This will ensure the maximum sale price for the lambs and thus maximise profit. 
Providing good nutrition over an extended period, however, requires good farm management 
skills as lambing time must be matched to a time of good pasture growth. Producers must be able to select appropriate pasture species and ensure adequate moisture and soil fertility.

Sample answer: 
Hybridisation of plants is the result of cross pollinating two different plant varietie s. This 
creates a new plant with a mix of genes that will grow more vigorously than its parents. 
Genetic engineering of plants involves modifying the DNA of a plant by inserting or editing 
DNA in the plant . This DNA could be from another organism totally unrelated to the plant that 
is being modified.

Sample answer: A technology used in the processing and marketing of milk is ultra-high-pressure 
pasteurisation. This inactivates bacteria in the milk and extends its shelf life . This process 
has minimal effect on the flavour, texture or nutritional value of the milk. The treated milk has 
the safety and health benefits of pasteurisation without any significant flavour change and so makes the milk more appealing to consumers and so i s more marketable.

also allows the farmer to sell the less desirable heifers before investing time and money into 
growing them out.

Question  26  (b)  
 Criteria  Marks 
 •     Provides an explanation of the relationship between rumen microbes and 
 ruminant digestion  4 
•  Describes  the  action  of  rumen  microbes  without  a  link  to  ruminant  
digestion  
OR  
•  Describes  some  aspect  of  ruminant  digestion  without  a  link  to  rumen  
microbes   3 
 •         Outlines some aspects of ruminant digestion involving microbes  2 
 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
Some rumen microbes act on carbohydrates including fibre, ingested by the ruminant, to 
obtain their energy needs. Byproducts of this include VFAs which are absorbed directly into 
the ruminant’s bloodstream , providing energy to the animal. Microbes can also use low grade 
protein or non- protein nitrogen entering the rumen to create their own bodily ‘microbial 
protein’. When microbes are later digested in the abomasum of the ruminant , this upgraded 
protein is available to the animal.

  performe d on the data gathered  3

 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
A test of significance should be performed to interpret experimental results. It can determine 
if the results are due to the treatment effect or from natural variation. 
Comparing the means of the treatments alone is not sufficient to identify a statistical 
difference. A test of significance also considers the spread of the data within each treatment.

Sample answer: 
A trial was carried out to assess the ability of ‘in field’ lateral flow strip assay to reliably detect 
such low levels of genetically modified (GM) canola. Mixing of GM and non- GM seed along 
the supply chain is difficult to avoid completely, so some is allowed. The EU set a threshold 
level for Australian imports at 0.9% GM seed for canola crop. 
The results of the trial in field conditions found that the lateral flow strip assay could find 
1 InVigor canola seed, via its expression of introduced PAT protein, in 50 non-GM seeds. 
Using SeedCalc software, the level of confidence of detection of GM seed at less than 0.9% 
in a non -GM sample was at 99%.

Sample answer: 
As the aim of this study was to validate sampling plans and test protocols for rapid, cost -
effective detection of GM canola seed using an immunoassay, the results showed that the 
lateral flow strips were successful in detecting GM canola at the required level in the field. 
The study did not investigate usual ‘in -field’ sampling plans however , and continued research

available lateral flow strip assay, and the suitability of other commercially available assays 
needs to be explored.

    variability or management strategies linked to climate variability  2

Sample answer: 
A trial was conducted to investigate the application of red sea -weeds (Asparagopsis sp) in 
mitigating methane production in grazing -based systems as a means to tackle climate 
change. 
The study found that ASP -Oil (Asparagopsis based edible -oil product) diets significantly 
decreased methane output in dairy cattle, when offered as an additive in their feeding 
system, compared to a non -ASP oil additive diet. Methane production (g/d) of cows given the 
ASP 1 diet (no seaweed biomass) was 44% lower than cows on the c ontrol diet and the 
ASP 2 diet (included seaweed biomass) was 31% lower. Neither compromised milk output or 
quality.

Sample answer: 
A notable challenge to the accuracy and reliability of UAV -based spectral -data acquisition in 
agricultural settings is the variation in ambient illumination . This study evaluated the 
performance of seven existing radiometric correction techniques to overcome this issue. All 
seven performed adequately in correcting distortion related to variation in ambient light, to 
ensure the accuracy of data collected. Of the seven, the Multiple Reference Panel (MRP) 
system performed the best when considering all criteria of accuracy, cost, time and 
consistency.
