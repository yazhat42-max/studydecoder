Question  2  
 Criteria  Marks 
 •        Explains effectively how Vanderbilt captures the experience of learning 
  as an adult , using well -chosen supporting evidence  4 
 •        Explains how Vanderbilt captures the experience of learning as an adult , 
 using appropriate supporting evidence  3 
 •       Describes how Vanderbilt captures the experience of learning as an 
  adult , using some supporting evidence  2 
 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
Vanderbilt conveys the experience of learning as an adult as more challenging than learning 
as a child. This is shown through the anecdote of parents mocking him for learning chess 
with his daughter and seeing ‘kids doing the activity, and adults like me staring into their 
smartphones.’ The question ‘Why are adults learning chess?’ asked in a ‘mocking tone’ 
suggests that it is unusual for children to see adults learning new skills. Vanderbilt’s anxiety 
about learning is evident in his internal reflection ‘I was nervous, even though I really had 
nothing to lose, sa ve my pride’, showing adults have a stronger fear of failure than children, 
making learning new skills more challenging. 
Question 3 
Criteria Marks 
• Explains effectively how Thompson represents the experience of being 
at the beach , using well -chosen supporting evidence 4 
• Explains how Thompson represents the experience of being at the 
beach, using appropriate supporting evidence 3 
• Describes how Thompson represents the experience of being at the 
beach, with some supporting evidence 2 
• Provides some relevant information about how Thompson represents the 
experience of being at the beach 1 
Sample answer: 
Thompson represents the experience of someone lying on the beach taking in their 
surroundings . ‘I spin/With nearing sleep’ metaphorically conveys the experience of the 
effects of the heat and surrendering to one’s senses. As the persona closes their eyes , they 
are ‘dissolved within’ , emphasising the power of the vivid experience. Thompson further 
represents the sensory experience of the beach through descriptions of the ‘thundering 
waves’ as the sunbather ‘shield [s]’ their eyes from the bright sunlight. Also, they are aware of 
the ‘lads and girls/ In dazzling colours’ who are enjoying themselves in the surf. The term 
‘oblivion’ suggests that the immensity of this moment could be absorbed into nothingness.

 •       Explains effectively how Pippos portrays Lucky ’s sense of hope for the 
   future, using well-chosen supporting evidence  4 
 •       Explains how Pippos portrays Lucky ’s sense of hope for the future , using 
  appropriate supporting evidence  3 
 •       Describes how Pippos portrays Lucky ’s sense of hope for the future , 
  using some supporting evidence  2 
 •     Provides some relevant information   1 
Sample answer: 
Pippos portrays Lucky ’s hope for the future through the metaphor of the ship as a ‘diorama of 
the world’, show ing that Lucky feels like the whole world is open to him. Lucky imagines Valia 
at various times of day, ‘working or eating ’, or ‘flicking up to distance a fly’, and Pippos states 
that for Lucky these are ‘pleasing scenarios ’. Lucky has ‘two gold rings hidden ’ which are 
symbolic of the love and promise of marriage and hope he feels for his future with Valia in 
Australia. Pippos describes Circular Qua y as ‘heavy with activity’ and the ‘northern shore in 
repose ’, the contrast between action and peace reflecting Lucky’s hope for his future success 
in both his business and his peaceful home with Valia . 
Question 5 
Criteria Marks 
• Explains in a highly effective manner how Fidge explores the impact of role models in her childhood , using detailed, well -chosen supporting 
evidence 5 
• Explains effectively how Fidge explores the impact of role models in her 
childhood, using well -chosen supporting evidence 4 
• Explains how Fidge explores the impact of role models in her childhood , 
with appropriate supporting evidence 3 
• Describes the impact of role models in her childhood 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample answer: 
Fidge explores the ‘monumental ’ impact that her favourite superhero, She- Ra, had in 
childhood through a strong, personal voice. She states that ‘She- Ra was made for me and 
my sister ’, demonstrating the importance of diverse gender representation of role models. 
When describing that they were ‘desperate to see … women doing anything remotely 
interesting ’, Fidge uses humour to emphasise the importance of seeing role models on 
television in childhood that resonated with her desire to see ‘strong women’ being 
represented doing aspirational things. When She-Ra was taken off the air, Fidge and her 
sister protest ed by writing ‘letters to the station to express our disappointment ’. Her letter is a 
small act of protest where she exclaims, ‘Put back She- Ra. Do it… NOW!! ’ where the 
imperative voice demonstrates the impact of having a superhero role model as it gave the 
author ongoing confidence to communicate her opinion with the hope of making change.
