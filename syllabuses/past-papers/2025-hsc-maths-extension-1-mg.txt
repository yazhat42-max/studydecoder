Question 12 (c)

CriteriaMarks• Provides correct proof3• Uses inductive assumption, or equivalent merit2• Establishes initial statement, or equivalent merit1Sample answer:

When n = 1
LHS = 1 × (1!) = 1
RHS = (1 + 1)! - 1 = 2 - 1 = 1
` True when n = 1

Assume true for n = k, that is

1 × (1!) + 2 × (2!) + · · · + k × (k!) = (k + 1)! - 1

Now prove true for n = k +1, that is prove
1 × (1!) + 2 × (2!) + · · · + k × (k!) + (k + 1) × ((k + 1)!) = (k + 2)! - 1
LHS = 61 × (1!) + 2 × (2!) + · · · + k × (k!)@ + (k + 1) × ((k + 1)!)
= 6(k + 1)! - 1@ + (k + 1) × ((k + 1)!) by assumption
= 61 + (k + 1)@ (k + 1)! - 1
= (k + 2)! - 1 = RHS.

Hence the statement is also true for n = k + 1. So the statement is true for all positive integers by mathematical induction.

Question 13 (b)

CriteriaMarks• Provides correct solution2• Obtains 7! unrestricted seating arrangements at a round table, or equivalent merit1Sample answer:

There are 7! possible seating arrangements at a round table without restriction. There are 6! × 2 possible seating arrangements where the two people sit together.
Hence, there are 7! - 6! × 2 = 3600 seating arrangements where they do not sit together.

Question 14 (a)

CriteriaMarks• Provides correct proof2• Creates 6 appropriate buckets, or equivalent merit1Sample answer:

Create 6 buckets 61, 60@, 62, 30@, 63, 20@, 64, 15@, 65, 12@, 66, 10@.
If we choose 7 numbers, then by the pigeonhole principle two numbers must be chosen from the same bucket. These two numbers multiply to 60. Hence, the product of all 7 numbers is a multiple of 60.
