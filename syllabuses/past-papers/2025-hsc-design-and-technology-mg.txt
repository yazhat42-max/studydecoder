      Question  13  
 Criteria  Marks 
•  Discusses  the  long -term environmental impact  of  the  battery power  
options   4 
•  Demonstrate s  an understanding of  the  long -term environmental impact  
of  the  battery power  option s   3 
•  Outlines  an  environmental  impact  of  battery power  options   2 
•  Provide  some  relevant  information   1 
 
 
 
  NESA 2025 HSC Design and Technology Marking Guidelines 
Sample  answer:  
When  considering  the  long- term consequences of the  battery power  options,  rechargeable 
batteries  have  less  impact  on  the  environment  long  term.  Rechargeable batteries  can be 
charged up and used multiple times compared to disposable batteries that  need to be 
disposed of  after  each power  cycle.   
 
However,  to  access  the  materials  required  for  production  of  rechargeable  batteries,  finite  
resources are required to be mined and refined in ways that can cause a negative  
environmental  impact . Growing consumer awareness of the possible environmental impact 
reduces customer acceptance of the product.  
Answers  could include:   
•  Less waste/landfill   
•  Increased demand for rare metals such as  lithium/ nickel  
•  Access  to  finite  resources  
•  Responsible  marketing  and  consumer  choice   
•  Battery  recycling centres  
•  Plastic  packaging
