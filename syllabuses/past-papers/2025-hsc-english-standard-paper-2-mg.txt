2025 HSC English Standard — Paper 2 
Marking Guidelines 
Section I  —  Module  A:  Language,  Identity  and  Culture  
Question 1 
Criteria Marks 
• Explains in a highly effective manner how the prescribed text reveals the 
benefits and challenges of belonging to specific cultures 
• Presents a highly effective response using relevant, detailed references 
to the prescribed text 
• Organises, develops and expresses ideas effectively using language appropriate to audience, purpose and form 17–20 
• Explains effectively how the prescribed text reveals the benefits and 
challenges of belonging to specific cultures 
• Presents an effective response using relevant references to the 
prescribed text 
• Organises, develops and expresses ideas competently using language appropriate to audience, purpose and form 13–16 
• Explains competently how the prescribed text reveals the benefits and/or 
challenges of belonging to specific cultures 
• Presents a competent response using adequate references to the 
prescribed text 
• Organises, develops and expresses ideas adequately using language appropriate to audience, purpose and form 9–12 
• Describes aspects of the prescribed text with some reference to the 
question 
• Presents a response using limited reference to the prescribed text 
• Attempts to organise a response in a limited way 5–8 
• Attempts to describe aspects of the text 
• Attempts to organise a response in an elementary way 1–4

Section III  —  Module  C:  The  Craft  of  Writing  
Question 3 
Criteria Marks 
• Composes a highly engaging imaginative or discursive piece of writing 
that effectively uses the stimulus 
• Crafts language skilfully to capture the richness of a fond memory 
• Demonstrates highly effective control of language and structure appropriate to audience, purpose, context and selected form 17–20 
• Composes an engaging imaginative or discursive piece of writing that 
effectively uses the stimulus 
• Crafts language effectively to capture the richness of a fond memory 
• Demonstrates effective control of language and structure appropriate to audience, purpose, context and selected form 13–16 
• Composes an imaginative or discursive piece of writing that uses the 
stimulus 
• Uses language competently to capture the richness of a fond memory 
• Demonstrates sound control of language and structure appropriate to 
audience, purpose, context and selected form 9–12 
• Composes a piece of writing that attempts to respond to the stimulus 
• Demonstrates variable control of language and structure 5–8 
• Attempts to compose a piece of writing with some relevance to the
