• Outlines the use of diodes in rectification of the AC current 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
Diodes  convert  alternating  current  (AC)  to  direct  current  (DC).  They  allow current  to  flow only  
in  one  direction,  blocking  the  reverse  flow.  This  results  in  the  conversion  of  AC,  which  
oscillates in both directions to DC,  which flows in a single direction.  
Answers  could include:  
•  Diodes  allow  current  to  flow  in  only  one  direction  
•  AC is  converted  to  DC through  rectification  
•  Half-wave  and  full-wave  rectification  using  diodes  
•  Diagram s  howing  the  rectification  process  with  diodes  (if  applicable) .  
Question 12 
Criteria Marks 
• Distinguishes between a NOR gate and a NAND gate in a circuit 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
A NOR gate  produces  a  low  output  (0)  for  all  inputs except  when all  inputs are low, in which  
case the output  is high. A NAND gate produces a  high  output  (1)  for  all  inputs except  when 
all  inputs are high, in which case the output is  low.  
Answers  could include:  
•  NOR gate:  Output  is high  only when all  inputs are low; otherwise, output is  low.  
•  NAND gate:  Output  is low  only when all  inputs are high; otherwise, output is  high.

Sample  answer:  
Dielectric  strength  is  essential  to  prevent  electrical  breakdown,  but  material s  like  ceramics,  
while  strong,  can  be  brittle  and  hard  to  work  with.  Flexible  polymers  such  as  silicone  offer  
better  handling and moisture resistance,  though they may not  perform w  ell  under  extreme 
heat.  
 
Thermal  stability,  mechanical  durability  and  resistance  to  environmental  factors  like  UV 
exposure,  moisture  and  chemicals  are  vital,  especially  in  outdoor  or  industrial  settings.  It 
ensures consistent  performance and prevents degradation under  high temperatures 
generated during operation.  
 
Long- term performance including the ability to resist partial discharge is also important for 
system r  eliability.  Although cost  is often a consideration,  it  should not  outweigh safety and 
performance.   
Answers  could include:  
•  Dielectric  strength  and  its  importance  in  preventing  electrical breakdown  
•  Thermal  stability and the material’s ability to withstand high temperatures  
•  Need for  chemical  resistance to prevent  reactions with various substances  
•  Importance of mechanical strength for durability  
•  Need  for  moisture  resistance  to  maintain  insulating  properties  
•  Environmental  impact  and  safety  standards  compliance  
•  Cost-effectiveness and market  availability.

Legislation mandates that  companies responsible for  importing and manufacturing 
televisions, computers, and computer products must provide free recycling services for these  
items.  As  a  result,  the  electronics  industry  has  seen  a  significant  reduction  in  electronic 
waste  being  sent  to  landfills.  Companies  have  become  more  invested  in  designing  products  
that are easier to recycle and contain fewer hazardous materials. For example, many  
electronics manufacturers now use  recyclable materials and have implemented take- back 
programs to ensure proper  disposal  and recycling of  e-waste.  Regulations  have  greatly 
improved  the  industry ’s environmental  footprint  by promoting sustainable practices and 
reducing the amount of electronic waste.

Sample  answer:  
The  electronics  industry  has  been  significantly  affected by  various historical  developments in 
manufacturing  processes.  The  invention  of  the  transistor  in  1947  marked  a  revolutionary  
advancement,  leading to the miniaturis ation of  electronic components and the development  
of  smaller,  more efficient  devices.  This breakthrough paved the way for  the creation of  
modern  computers,  radios,  and  other  electronic  devices.  
 
In the 1960s, the development of integrated circuits (ICs) further transformed the industry. 
ICs allowed for the incorporation of multiple electronic components into a single chip, significantly increasing the functionality and complexity of  electronic dev ices.  This  
advancement  led to the creation of  more powerful  and compact  electronics,  such as personal  
computers and smartphones.  
 
The  rise  of  surface -mount  technology  (SMT)  in  the  late  20th  century  marked  another  
significant  milestone.  SMT enabl  ed the placement  of  electronic components directly onto the 
surface of  printed circuit  boards (PCBs),  increasing manufacturing efficiency and  reducing  
the size of electronic devices. This technology has allowed for the production of highly  
complex and compact  electronic devices that  are now ubi  quitous in everyday life.  
 
These  historical  advancements  have  collectively  enhanced  the  efficiency,  functionality,  and  
miniaturi sation of  electronic devices.  They have also opened up new possi  bilities for  
innovation  and  creativity,  shaping  the  electronics  industry  into  what  it  is  today.  
Answers  could include:  
•  Mechanisation  and  automation  revolutionised  production,  increasing  efficiency  and  
reducing labour costs.  
•  Mass  production  methods  enabled  affordability  and  wider  accessibility  of  products.  
•  Digital  and  computer -aided technologies enhanced precision,  consistency,  and design 
flexibility.

waste  and  errors.
