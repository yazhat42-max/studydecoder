• Outlines ONE way the electrical system of a motor vehicle is used to 
improve the safety of the vehicle ’s occupants 2 
• Provide s some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
Anti-lock  Braking  System  (ABS)  maintains  steering  control  during  braking  when  the  ABS 
sensors are electrically triggered.  
Answers  could include:  
•  The  electrical  system t  riggers  airbags  during  a  collision,  providing  a  cushion  that  reduces  
the impact on occupants and helps prevent serious injuries.  
•  The  Anti-lock  Braking  System  (ABS)  prevents wheel  lock-up during hard braking,  allowing 
the driver to maintain steering control and avoid obstacles.  
•  The  Electronic  Stability  Control  (ESC)  helps prevent  skidding and loss of  control  by 
automatically applying brakes to individual  wheels when it  detects a loss of  traction.  
•  Properly  functioning  headlights,  taillights,  and  indicators  ensure  that  the  vehicle  is  visible  
to other road users, reducing the risk of accidents, especially in low visibility conditions.

Sample  answer:  
The  automotive  industry  has  been  significantly  affected by  various historical  developments i
manufacturing  processes.  The  introduction  of  the  assembly  line  by  Henry  Ford  in  the  early  
20th century revolutioni sed the industry.  This innovation allowed for  mass production of  
vehicles,  significantly reducing production costs and making cars more affordable to the 
general  public.  
 
In the mid- 20th century,  the development  of  automation and robotics in manufacturing 
processes further  transformed the industry.  These advancements increased production 
efficiency and precision while reducing labour  costs and errors.  Automated assembly lines 
enabled the production of  high- quality vehicles at  a faster  rate,  meeting the growing deman
for automobiles.  
 
The  rise  of  computer -aided design (CAD)  and computer -aided manufacturing (CAM)  in the 
late  20th  century  marked  another  significant  milestone.  These  technologies  allowed  for  mor
accurate and efficient  design and production processes.  CAD and  CAM syst  ems enabled 
automotive manufacturers to create complex vehicle components with greater  precision,  
enhancing the overall  quality and performance of  vehicles.  
 
These  historical  advancements  have  collectively  improved  the  efficiency,  quality,  and  
affordability of  automotive production.  They have also opened up new possi  bilities for  
innovation  and  design,  shaping  the  automotive  industry  into  what  it  is  today.
