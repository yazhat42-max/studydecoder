Sample answer: 
The  Si berian  Traps  were  formed  by  massive  outpouring  of  lava  over  millions  of  years.  These  
eruptions produced huge amounts of  SO2  and CO2. Following the initial cooling caused by  
SO2, the amount of CO2  emissions created significant  long-term warming. The size of the  
Pinatubo  eruption  was  small  in  comparison  and  the  most  significant  impact  on  climate  from  
Pinatubo  was  a  slight  global  cooling.  Unlike  the  Siberian  Trap  eruptions,  the  impact  on  global  
warming  caused  by  Pinatubo  was  small  due  to the limited release of  CO2  in  the  eruption.

Sample answer: 
A tsunami is caused by the displacement of water in the ocean. The tsunami has a 
wavelength of 280 km in deep water, as shown by Figures 1 and 2. As the ocean floor

to infrastructure and harm people, hence its classification as a disaster.

Sample answer: 
Anthropogenic  cl imate  change  is  caused  by  activities  such  as  burning  coal  to  generate  
electricity,  which introduces excess CO2  into  the  atmosphere.  CO2  traps the Sun’s heat 
causing global  warming.  As native trees grow, they photosynthesise, capturing CO2  from the  
atmosphere and locking the carbon in their  wood.  Trees draw w  ater  from t he soil  by 
transpiration, which draws heat from surrounding air to evaporate water from the tree’s  
leaves.  This  cools  the  air  around  the  tree,  reducing  local warmer  temperatures.  
 
Mines  produce  large  quantities  of  tailings,  waste  material  which  can  escape  causing  dust  
pollution in the air  and contaminating waterways.  Tailings are stored in dams.  During  mine  
reclamation th  e tailings dam is  covered in topsoil  and trees are planted over  the dam.  Tree 
roots stabilise the soil, which contains the tailings by preventing erosion. Choosing  native  
trees also provides habitat.   
 
Planting  deep  rooted  native  trees  is  very  effective  in  combating cl  imate change   because they 
capture CO2, act  as  a  carbon si  nk  and  lower  local temperatures.  Trees  successfully  contain  
mine  tailings  protecting  the  surrounding  environment.

•  Provides  a  reason  for  the  breakup  of  a  supercontinent  
OR  
•  Provides  a  reason  for  the  formation  of  a  supercontinent   2–3 
 •     Provides some relevant information  1 
Sample answer: 
The supercontinent Rodinia insulated the mantle, allowing heat to build up. Hot magma 
became less dense and rose, causing Rodinia to rift (1). As the rift widened, new oceanic 
crust filled the gap and a new ocean basin formed with a mid ocean ridge at its centre (2) 
and small continents were separated by the new oceans. 
The small continents continued to drift until they started to converge again. As small 
continents approach ed each other, oceanic crust subducted (3). When the ocean basin 
eventually closed between the continents, the continents collided (4) forming the new 
supercontinent Pang aea.
