Question 14 
Criteria Marks 
• Describes the process of producing an internal thread on a cylindrical 
metal shape , using a lathe 4 
• Outlines the process of producing an internal thread on a cylindrical 
metal shape , using a lathe 3 
• Demonstrates some understanding of the process of producing an 
internal thread 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
Mark  the  centre  of  the  shape  that  an  internal  thread  is  to  be  produced.  Select  the  correct  size  
tapping drill and then drill a hole to the required depth. To produce the thread ,  a set  of  taps,  
taper, intermediate and plug are required.  Secure  the  workpiece  in  the  chuck  of  the  lathe.  
Use  a  cutting  compound  paste  to  assist  in  the  process.  
 
Turn  on  the  lathe,  using  a  drill  bit  to  drill  the  pilot  hole.  Remove  the  drill  bit  and  move  the  
tailstock forward to cut the thread.  
 
To  begin  tapping,  rotate  the  taper  bit  clockwise  until  you  feel  resistance  from t he  threads  
cutting into the material.  From t  here,  for  every full  rotation clockwise,  make a half  rotation 
counterclockwise.  Alternate the use of  the full  set  of  taps.   
Answers  could include:  
Tapping  drill, tap wrench,  taper  tap,  intermediate tap,  plug tap,  cutting compound and drilling.

Question 15 
Criteria Marks 
• Compares the use of hollow section steel tube and the use of solid steel 
section 5 
• Describes the use of hollow section steel tube and the use of solid steel 
section 4 
• Outlines the use of hollow section steel tube and the use of solid steel 
section 3 
• Demonstrates some understanding o f the use of hollow section steel 
tube and solid steel section 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
Compared  to  solid  sections,  tube  steel  offers  a  higher  strength -to-weight  ratio  due  to  its  
hollow desi  gn.  This makes it  ideal  for  applications where weight  reduction is crucial  while 
maintaining  structural  integrity.  
 
Solid  sections  are  better  suited  for  situations  where  maximum c  ompressive  strength  is  
needed,  and weight  is less of  a concern.  
 Tube  steel  is  also  more  versatile  in  terms  of  shape  and  can  be  easily  worked  into  a  range  of  
shapes for  complex structures,  whereas solid sections are typically more limited in their  
design flexibility.  
Answers  could include:  
Higher  weight  to  strength  ration,  more  versatile  in  application,  tube  steels  can  be  
manufactured  in  a  range  of  shapes,  can  be  easily  reworked.

Sample  answer:  
The  metal  and  engineering  industry  has  been  significantly  affected by  various historical  
developments in manufacturing processes.  The introduction of  the Bessemer  process in the 
mid-19th century revolutioni sed steel  production by making it  more efficient  and 
cost- effective.  This development  allowed for  the mass production of  steel,  which became a 
crucial  material  in construction,  transportation,  and manufacturing.  
 
In the early 20th  century,  the development  of  assembly line production,  pioneered by 
Henry  Ford,  further  transformed  the  industry.  This  innovation  significantly  increased  
production efficiency and reduced costs,  allowing for  the mass production of  metal  products 
and machinery.  
 
The  advent  of  computer  numerical  control  (CNC)  technology  in  the  late  20th  century marked 
another  significant  advancement.  CNC m  achines enabled highly precise and automated 
machining  processes,  reducing  labour  costs  and  improving  product  quality.  This  technology  
has allowed for  greater  customis ation and innovation in metal  and engineering designs,  
catering to diverse market  demands.  
 
These  historical  advancements  have  collectively  enhanced  the  efficiency,  quality,  and  
affordability of  metal  and engineering production.  They have also opened up new possi  bilities 
for innovation and creativity, shaping the industry into what it is today.  
Answers  could include:  
•  Mechanisation  and  automation  revolutionised  production,  increasing  efficiency  and  
reducing labour costs.  
•  Mass  production  methods  enabled  affordability  and  wider  accessibility  of  products.  
•  Digital  and  computer -aided technologies enhanced precision,  consistency,  and design 
flexibility.  
•  Miniaturisation  of  components  improved  functionality,  portability,  and  material  efficiency.
