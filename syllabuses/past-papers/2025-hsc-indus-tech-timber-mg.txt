Question 14 
Criteria Marks 
• Provides a thorough description of a suitable bending process 4 
• Provides some description of a suitable bending process 3 
• Demonstrates some understanding of a bending process 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample answer: 
To  c reate  a  curved  feature  leg  for  the  table,  you  can  use  the  laminating  process.  Start  by  
cutting thin strips of  wood that  can bend easily.  Apply a strong adhesive to each strip and 
stack them t  ogether.  Place the stacked strips onto a pre -made  form with  the desired curve 
and secure them w  ith clamps.  Allow t  he adhesive to cure completely,  ensuring the layers 
bond together.  Once the adhesive is fully set,  remove the clamps and sand the curved leg to 
smooth out  any rough edges.  
Answers  could include:  
•  Steam  bending:  soaking wood in water,  steaming it  to increase pliability,  bending around a 
form and clamping to set the shape.  
•  Kerf  bending:  making a series of  cuts (kerfs)  on the inside of  the curve to allow t  he wood  
to bend, then filling the kerfs with adhesive and clamping to set the shape.

• Provides a thorough description of appropriate set-up, startup and 
maintenance procedures for operating high -risk machinery 5 
• Describe s appropriate set-up, startup and maintenance procedures for 
operating high- risk machinery 4 
• Outlines some of the set- up, startup and /or maintenance procedures for 
operating high- risk machinery 3 
• Outlines ONE set-up or startup or maintenance procedure for operating 
high-risk machinery 2 
• Provides some relevant information 1 
Sample  answer:  
First,  the  machine  should be checked for any damage and  to  make  sure  all  safety  guards  are  
in  place.  For  startup,  test  the  controls  and  safety  features  and  make  sure  all the  necessary  
protective gear  are worn. For  maintenance,  regularly  inspect  and  lubricate  the  machine,  
replace any worn -out  parts and keep a detailed maintenance log.  Clean the machine often to 
prevent  dust  and debris from bui  lding up.  
Answer  could include:  
•  Specific  setup p rocedures: ensuring proper  ventilation,  checking for  debris and securing 
the work area.  
•  Pre-operation checks:  verifying power  supply,  ensuring all  machine guards are in place 
and checking that  emergency stop mechanisms are functioning.  
•  Operator  training: ensuring operators are adequately trained and understand the 
operation manual.  
•  Routine  maintenance:  scheduling regular  maintenance checks,  including inspecting belts,  
blades and other  moving parts.  
•  Lubrication:  ensuring all  moving parts are properly lubricated to prevent  wear  and tear.  
•  Cleaning:  regularly cleaning the machinery to prevent  dust  and debris buildup,  which can 
affect  machine performance.  
•  Record  keeping:  keeping detailed records of  all  maintenance activities,  repairs and 
inspections.  
•  Emergency  procedures: having clear  emergency procedures in place,  including knowing 
how t o quickly shut  down the machine in case of  a malfunction.

Sample  answer:  
The  woodworking  industry  has  been  significantly  affected by  various historical  developments 
in  manufacturing  processes.  During  the  Industrial Revolution,  the  introduction  of  steam -
powered machinery drastically increased production capabilities and efficiency.  This allowed 
for the mass production of wooden furniture and other products, meeting the growing  
demands of  the time.  
 
In the 20th  century,  the advent  of  electric -powered tools,  such as electric saws and lathes,  
further revolutioni sed the industry.  These tools improved precision and reduced the physical  
labour  required  for  woodworking,  allowing  for  more  intricate  and  consistent  designs.  
Additionally,  the  standardi sation of  parts and materials during this period led to greater  
uniformity and quality control  in products.  
 
The  introduction  of  computer  numerical  control  (CNC)  technology  in  the  late  20th  century 
marked  a  significant  advancement.  CNC  machines  enabled  highly  precise  and  complex  cuts,  
which  were  previously  impossible  with  manual  tools.  This  technology  has  allowed  for  greater  
customis ation and innovation in woodworking designs,  catering to diverse consumer  
demands.  
 
These  historical  developments  have  collectively  enhanced  the  productivity,  safety  and  
precision of  the woodworking industry.  They have also opened up new possi  bilities for  design 
and creativity,  shaping the industry into what  it  is today.  
Answers  could include:  
•  Mechanisation  and  automation  revolutionised  production,  increasing  efficiency  and  
reducing labour costs.  
•  Mass  production  methods  enabled  affordability  and  wider  accessibility  of  products.  
•  Digital  and  computer -aided technologies enhanced precision,  consistency and design 
flexibility.

waste  and  errors.
