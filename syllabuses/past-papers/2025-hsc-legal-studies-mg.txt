Section II 
Part A — Human Right s 
Question 21 
Criteria Marks 
• Demonstrates a sound understanding of how the recognition of peace 
rights has changed over time 3 
• Demonstrates some understanding of how peace rights changed over 
time 2 
• Provides some relevant information about peace rights 1 
Sample answer: 
Peace rights are the collective, third generation right to live free from conflict which has 
evolved over time. Under the UN Charter 1945 , governments are required to take necessary 
action to avoid war. This responsibility has evolved into a more significant obligation to recognise and act to work towards maintaining peace for all peoples, as seen in the Declaration of Rights of People to Peace 1984 . But this is not always achieved e g 
Gaza/Ukraine. 
Question 22 
Criteria Marks 
• Provides a thorough explanation of how the media influences changes in 
human rights 
• Integrates relevant example s 5 
• Provides a sound explanation of how the media influences changes in 
human rights 
• Refers to relevant example s 4 
• Provides some explanation of how the media influences changes in 
human rights 
• May use an example 3 
• Makes general statements about how the media influences changes in 
human rights 2 
• Provides some relevant information about human rights 1 
Sample answer: 
The media has a key role in influencing change in human rights because it provides a 
platform for raising awareness and advocating for action on issues of community concern. 
Whilst press freedom is a generally widely accepted right in democracies, it is not universally 
protected. For example, some countries impose media censorship on the reporting of issues. The media also has a significant responsibility t o inform the public about human rights 
violations and inadequacies of existing laws e g treatment of asylum seekers. In this way, the 
media influences c hange in human rights. It acts as an important mechanism for ensuring 
accountability and transparency of law -making bodies and prompts the reform process e g 
Four Corners 2024 ‘Closure of Don Dale detention centre in the NT’ .
