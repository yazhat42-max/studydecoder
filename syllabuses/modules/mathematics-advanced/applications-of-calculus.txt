Real-world applications
Integration is used to find the average value of a function, the length of a curve or the surface area of a solid.

Simpson’s rule uses a quadratic equation through three given points to estimate the area under a curve.

Teaching advice
Purpose
Calculus involves the study of how things change and provides a framework for developing quantitative models of change and deducing their consequences. It explores the development of analytic and numeric integration techniques and the use of these techniques in solving problems.
The study of calculus is important in developing students’ knowledge, understanding and capacity to operate with and model situations involving change. It also supports students to use algebraic and graphical techniques to describe and solve problems and to predict future outcomes with relevance to science, engineering, finance, economics and the construction industry.
